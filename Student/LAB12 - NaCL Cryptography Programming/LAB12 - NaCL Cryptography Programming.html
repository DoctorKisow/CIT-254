Welcome to the Scripting Lab, an integral component of our comprehensive cybersecurity curriculum. In this lab, students will delve into at least two scripting frameworks. The primary objective of this lab is to familiarize you with two scripting frameworks of your choice. This lab is aligned with Perform Data Analysis, Systems Analysis, and Risk Assessments, where students will gain firsthand experience in leveraging scripting for data and systems analysis, essential components of comprehensive risk assessments within the information security domain, Defend Against Information System Attacks, illustrating how scripting plays a pivotal role in building defensive mechanisms, and Configure and Apply Information Security Tools, which enhances students' proficiency in configuring and applying information security tools.

Through this lab, students will develop tools for tasks like network discovery, vulnerability scanning, and more by employing scripting frameworks and acquiring skills to create customized scripts that contribute to a robust defense against information system attacks. By the end of this lab, students will have a deeper understanding of scripting frameworks and a practical skill set that aligns with the demands of the cybersecurity landscape.

Part 2 - Writing a Script

  1. Using your book and the Internet as a reference, choose two of the scripting languages from Part 1 and create the following script(s):

    1. It must ask you for your weight on the earth.
    2. It must contain at least one constant: the moon's gravity, 1.625.
    3. It must contain at least one variable.
    4. It must calculate your weight on the moon based on your entered earth weight.
    5. It must display your weight on the moon, rounding to two decimal places.

     

    DELIVERABLE
      2.1
      A.

    A copy of the script in its final format.

    When your script is copied and pasted into Linux; it should run as intended.
      B.

    A screenshot of the running script that shows two output sets, one at your weight and another at 170 pounds.
      C.

    Write a detailed one- to two-paragraph explanation in your own words, as if you were explaining it to another student that details the logic of your two scripts.
